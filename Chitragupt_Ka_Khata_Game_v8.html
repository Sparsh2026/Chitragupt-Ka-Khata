Chitragupt is Yamraj's accountant — the keeper of the khata, the record of every soul's deeds and wishes. He is meticulous, slightly flustered (there are a lot of souls), and takes his register very seriously.

What is a Khata?
Every Indian household once had a khata — the shopkeeper's ledger where every transaction was recorded. Nothing forgotten. The word carries three meanings, and all three are at the heart of this game:
  • 1.The account book — every act of your life, recorded since the day you were born.
  • 2.The tab — what is owed, what has been given, what remains unsettled.
  • 3.The mistake — yeh meri khata hai. The things you would like to address before the final accounting.
What is a Living Will?
What is it?
A Living Will — also called an Advance Medical Directive (AMD) — is a simple document where you write down your medical wishes for a time when you may not be able to speak for yourself. It answers: if I am very ill and cannot decide, what do I want?
Why does it matter?
Without a Living Will, your family must guess what you would want — often in the middle of a crisis, when they are already grieving. Families argue. Doctors default to doing everything. The result is often more suffering, not less. Your Living Will is the gift of clarity.
Is it legal in India?
Yes. The Supreme Court of India made it legal in 2018 and simplified the process in 2023. You do not need a lawyer or a magistrate. Two witnesses and a notary are enough. Any adult with decision-making capacity can write one.
Who needs one?
Everyone. Not just the elderly or the ill. Accidents and sudden illness can happen at any age. Writing a Living Will is not a preparation for death — it is an act of love for the people who will have to make decisions for you.
Important: What Indian law says
A valid AMD takes legal priority over family wishes. Doctors who follow a patient's AMD in good faith are protected from prosecution (Supreme Court, 2018). Withholding a ventilator per a patient's AMD is not euthanasia — it is allowing natural death. Pain relief — even strong medication — is legally permitted when comfort is the primary goal.
What to write in your Living Will
1
Your values. What matters most to you — being free from pain, being at home, staying conscious, having your rituals observed? Start here, before any medical decisions.
2
Your treatment preferences. Do you want CPR if your heart stops? A ventilator if you cannot breathe? A feeding tube? These are medical procedures — you have the right to say yes or no to each one.
3
Your healthcare representative. Name one person who knows your wishes and will speak for you. Make sure they have agreed, and make sure the rest of your family knows who they are.
4
Where to keep it. Give a copy to your doctor, one to your healthcare representative, and one to a trusted family member. An AMD no one can find does not protect you.

What is a Khata?
In every Indian household, there was once a khata — the shopkeeper's ledger, the family account book. Nothing was forgotten. Every debt, every credit, every favour given and received.
Chitragupt — the divine scribe of Hindu cosmology, accountant to Yamraj, keeper of all earthly records — has one too. Your khata. Every act of your life, recorded since the day you arrived.
Khata (खाता) — three meanings at once:
  • The account book — every transaction of your life, recorded.
  • The tab — what is owed, what has been given, what remains unsettled.
  • The mistake — yeh meri khata hai. The things you'd like to address before the final accounting.
This game is not about dying. It is about what goes into the khata while you are still writing it — what you value, what you want, who you trust, and what you want your family to know.
